Welcome. My name is Juan Carlos Urcia Barea.


I am pursuing a Master in Public Affairs at Princeton University.


I most recently served as a Legislative Correspondent for US Senator Ben Cardin. My job was to help advance the Senator's legislative priorities and oversee policy on labor, technology, immigration, and the social safety net. This saw me develop and organize numerous bills and oversight letters, in addition to recommendations for cosponsorship, votes, and background memos for high level meetings and committee hearings.


Politics, philosophy, and economics are my passions. I am interested in the evolving relationship between the state and individuals and markets, as well as the role of the state in the international system. How can certain economic and labor policies create a society that is both more equitable and strategic for the modern world? Are certain economic realities "inevitable" or open to political choice?


I completed my Bachelor of Arts in History at Brown University, focusing on Ancient Rome and Late Antiquity.


My long-term objective is to build an impactful career in public service while also learning more about the country and the world one step at a time. It has been a very fulfilling experience so far.
